Foods guide
What to eat freely and what to avoid with rheumatoid arthritis
✅ Anti-inflammatory stars
Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, trout, mackerel), walnuts, flaxseeds, chia seeds, extra virgin olive oil, blueberries, tart cherries, strawberries, turmeric, ginger, garlic, spinach, kale, broccoli, sweet potato, quinoa, brown rice, lentils, chickpeas, green tea, avocado, pumpkin seeds.
🚫 Avoid or limit
Refined sugar and sweets, gluten (wheat, barley, rye), dairy, red and processed meats, trans fats, vegetable seed oils (sunflower, corn), alcohol, excess salt, processed packaged foods. Nightshade vegetables in excess during flares (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, potatoes).
💊 Nutrient priorities
Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA & DHA), Vitamin D, Vitamin C, Magnesium, Zinc, Curcumin (from turmeric), Selenium, Folate, Antioxidants (polyphenols, anthocyanins). Aim for a rainbow of plant foods daily.
🩺 Important notes
Omega-3 foods may interact with blood thinners — consult her rheumatologist. Vitamin D supplementation is often needed. Some RA medications require monitoring certain foods. Always review major dietary changes with her doctor.
